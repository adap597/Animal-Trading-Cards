Snow Leopard

The snow leopard's tail is used for fat storage, which allows them to survive during periods of scarcity.

  • Scientific Name: Panthera uncia
  • Average Weight: 55 to 165 lbs
  • Average Lifespan: 15 to 18 years in the wild or 25 years in captivity
  • Habitat: Alpine regions in central and south Asia

Snow leopards are smaller than most other big cats. Their large paws allow them to move stealthily and easily over snow. Their long thick tails, which can be up to a meter in length, allow them to balance easily while traversing their high-altitude alpine environment. Unfortunately, these beautiful and elusive cats have declined in numbers due in part to habitat loss. They are considered a vulnerable species.
